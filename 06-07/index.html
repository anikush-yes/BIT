Facts about forests

Forests are essential ecosystems that play a crucial role in the health of the planet. Here are some key facts about forests

Types of Forests

Tropical forests: Found near the equator, known for their biodiversity. Example: the Amazon Rainforest. Temperate forests: Located in regions with distinct seasons. Example: forests in North America and Europe. Boreal forests (Taiga): Found in cold northern regions. Example: forests in Canada and Russia

Biodiversity

Forests are home to over 80% of terrestrial animals, plants, and fungi. Tropical rainforests contain the highest level of biodiversity. Some species are only found in specific forests

Water Cycle

Forests help regulate the Earth's water cycle by absorbing rainfall, preventing erosion, and maintaining water supplies for nearby rivers and lakes. They also generate moisture through transpiration, influencing weather patterns

Human Dependence

Over 1.6 billion people worldwide depend on forests for food, fresh water, clothing, traditional medicine, and shelter. Forests provide numerous resources, including timber, paper, and non-timber products like fruits, nuts, and medicinal plants

Deforestation and Habitat Loss

Deforestation, primarily driven by agriculture, logging, and urbanization, leads to loss of biodiversity and contributes to climate change. Around 10 million hectares of forest are lost annually (as of 2020), despite efforts to promote conservation and reforestation

Oxygen Production

Forests contribute significantly to the production of oxygen through photosynthesis. Tropical forests, in particular, are vital "lungs of the planet"
